Lithium Industrialization: Strategic Pillar for Bolivia's Energy Sovereignty

Bolivia has one of the world's largest lithium reserves. Vertical integration—from responsible extraction to battery production—represents a structural solution to fossil fuel shortages and an opportunity for sustainable economic development.

See the Strategies

About the Initiative

Lithium—known as "white gold"—is the critical raw material for rechargeable batteries powering electric vehicles, stationary storage systems, and electronic devices. The proposed strategy seeks to transform extraction into high value-added production chains, mitigating environmental impacts and prioritizing social benefits.

Main objectives:

  • Create integrated industrial complexes for cell production and battery assembly.
  • Promote public and private electric mobility with incentives and fleet renewal programs.
  • Foster research, technical training, and technology transfer through international partnerships.

Proposed Solutions and Key Strategies

To turn the resource into sustainable development, we propose three complementary pillars:

Integrated Industrial Complexes

Development of plants integrating refining, cathode production, cell manufacturing, and final assembly, prioritizing local suppliers and supply chain control.

Electric Mobility and Storage

Tax incentives for public and private fleets, charging infrastructure, and urban transport renewal programs using locally produced batteries.

Innovation Partnerships

Agreements with universities and technology centers for R&D, specialized training, and standardization of manufacturing and recycling processes.
